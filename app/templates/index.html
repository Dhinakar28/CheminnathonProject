      What does "Top Predicted Failure Mode" mean?

      This dashboard shows the most frequently predicted failure mode from the classifier when applied to the uploaded dataset.

      Each mode is a short label describing a likely cause of failure detected by the model. Below are plain-English explanations:

      • Tool Wear Failure: The tool's cutting surface is worn; performance decreases and risk of failure rises.
      • Heat Dissipation Failure: Excessive heat build-up, cooling issues or poor thermal management.
      • Power Failure: Problems with electrical supply or unstable power causing faults.
      • Overstrain Failure: Mechanical overloading or stress causing premature damage.
      • Random Failure: Irregular faults not clearly attributable to a single mode.
      • bearing: Bearing degradation or imminent bearing failure.
      • wear: General mechanical wear across parts.
      • electrical: Electrical subsystem faults.

      Use the counts and percentage next to the mode to understand how prevalent the predicted mode is across the dataset.
